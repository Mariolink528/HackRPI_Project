The Sun

  • In 2023, India’s Aditya-L1 and ESA’s Proba-3 solar observatories are being launched to study the Sun’s corona.

Venus

  • India’s Shukrayaan orbiter is aimed to be sent in December 2024 to map the surface.
  • VERITAS and DAVINCI are being launched by NASA no earlier than 2027 and 2029 to map the planet’s topography and explore its atmosphere and geological features.
  • ESA’s EnVision is launching no earlier than 2031 to observe the atmosphere and overall structure of the planet.

Earth

  • NASA’s ACS3 (solar sail spacecraft) will be sent into orbit no earlier than 2023.
  • NASA’s Solar Cruiser is being launched in 2025 to create an artificial orbit between the Earth and the Sun.
  • In 2026, NASA’s NEO Surveyor is being launched to find 90% of asteroids large enough to destroy a city.

The Moon

  • Artemis I of NASA’s Artemis program is being launched in 2022.
  • By the end of 2022, ispace Japan is sending its first Moon landing mission with several international payloads onboard.
  • In 2024, NASA’s VIPER rover will go to the Moon’s south pole to study water ice.
  • NASA is also aiming to send payloads to the Moon and broaden our understanding of water on the Moon.

Mars

  • In 2024, Japan’s Martian Moons eXploration mission is being sent to collect samples of Phobos.
  • ESCAPADE twin orbiters will also be launched in 2024 to study the planet’s atmosphere and past.
  • ESA is sending the ExoMars rover to find signs of life on Mars by 2030.

Jupiter

  • ESA will launch JUICE in 2022 to explore the planet and its moons – Europa, Callisto, and Ganymede.
  • NASA will send the Europa Clipper in 2024 to see whether the moon Europa could support life.

Saturn

  • Dragonfly is being launched by NASA in 2027 to explore the planet’s moon Titan.

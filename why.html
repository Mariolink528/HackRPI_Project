By pushing past our boundaries in space exploration, we are able to question the theories that we believe are true and find answers to questions we don’t know: Can we apply the fundamental theories that work on Earth on other planets? Are we alone in this universe? The latter is a question that has pondered scientists for years and when water was found on Mars, the possibility of finding another intelligent species like us increased significantly. Until now, this species has yet to be discovered but with the advancement of technology, we will be able to locate more Earth-like planets and one day visit them in-person.

In addition to expanding our knowledge on the universe, space exploration serves as a way for countries all over the world to combine resources and work together. Over the years, countries improved space equipment and sent out probes and spacecrafts to collect data on the celestial bodies within our solar system. As global warming becomes an increasingly pressing issue, the images and temperature measurements collected from telescopes and probes can be used to urge world leaders into taking more steps to reduce the numerous amounts of greenhouse gasses being emitted into the atmosphere. And if that doesn’t sound important to you, space exploration also creates tons of jobs. NASA alone supports nearly 340,000 jobs nationwide. Imagine how many jobs space companies support altogether!
